Title: The Story of Prophet Musa (Moses) and the Sea

Prophet Musa stood at the edge of the Red Sea, with the army of Pharaoh closing in behind him. His people were terrified. There was no way forward, no way back. In that moment of complete pressure, Musa didn't panic. He didn't try to fight the army with weapons he didn't have. He didn't try to build a boat. He simply trusted. Then, the sea split—not all at once, but gradually, as if the water was learning to step aside. And his people walked through, step by step, on dry ground.

Think about what Musa and his people faced. They had no control over the sea. They had no control over the army. The only thing they could control was their next step—one foot in front of the other. They didn't look at the towering walls of water on both sides. They didn't stop to count the waves. They just kept moving forward. The path appeared as they walked, not before. The sea didn't part all the way to the other side instantly—it opened as they moved.

Now imagine you're reading a difficult text. The words feel like a wall of water. The army of distractions is behind you. You can't control the complexity of the text. You can't control how tired you are. But you can control your next step. You can focus on the next sentence, the next paragraph. You don't need to understand the entire text at once. You just need to move forward, line by line. Understanding doesn't always come before reading—it comes during reading.

Musa didn't wait for the sea to part completely before he started walking. He started walking, and the sea parted as he moved. Speed reading works the same way. Don't wait until you feel completely ready. Don't wait until you understand every word before you move on. Start reading. The understanding will come as you go. The path appears beneath your feet, not before them. And when you look back, you'll realize that the water—the confusion, the complexity—was never as tall as it seemed.
