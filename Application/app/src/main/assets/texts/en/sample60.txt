Title: The Boy Who Read Too Slowly

A boy named Sam loved stories but read very slowly. He would sound out every word, letter by letter. His teacher gave him a book with a timer and said, "Read as fast as you can, but don't stop when you don't understand a word." Sam was nervous. He started reading, but when he reached a word he didn't know, he almost stopped. He remembered the teacher's instruction and kept going. He read the next sentence, then the next. Three sentences later, he realized he understood the word. The meaning had appeared later in the text.

Sam finished the page faster than he ever had. He didn't understand every word, but he understood the story. He tried the same method with the next page, then the next. Each time, he found that difficult words became clear after a few more sentences. He learned that reading is not about understanding everything immediately—it's about trusting that understanding will come as you move forward. By the end of the week, Sam's speed had doubled. He didn't read every letter anymore. He read for meaning.
