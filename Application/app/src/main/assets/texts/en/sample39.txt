Title: The Importance of Hydration

Your brain is about 73% water. Even mild dehydration—losing just 1-2% of your body's water—can affect cognitive function, memory, and focus. Studies show that dehydrated individuals perform worse on reading comprehension tests and take longer to process information. The brain, like a machine, needs proper fluid to work efficiently. Speed reading is impossible when your brain is running on empty.

What does water have to do with reading speed? Everything. When you're dehydrated, your eyes get tired faster. Your concentration drops. Words blur. You re-read sentences. A glass of water before reading can sharpen your vision and calm your nervous system. It's one of the simplest ways to improve reading speed, yet most people ignore it.

Here's a practical tip: keep a glass or bottle of water on your desk while reading. Take small sips regularly. This not only hydrates you but also gives your brain tiny breaks—a moment to pause and reset. These micro-breaks prevent fatigue and keep your reading rhythm steady. Professional speed readers often use this trick to maintain high focus over long periods.

The next time you sit down to read, start by drinking a glass of water. Notice how your mind feels clearer, your eyes sharper, and your attention more steady. Reading is not just an act of the eyes—it's an act of a well-hydrated brain. Give your brain the water it needs, and your speed will naturally increase.
