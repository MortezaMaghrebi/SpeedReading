Title: The Innovation of Apple

Apple didn't invent the computer, the smartphone, or the tablet. But they revolutionized all of them. Their secret wasn't just technology—it was simplicity. Steve Jobs believed that "design is not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works." Apple stripped away clutter, removed unnecessary buttons, and made products intuitive. A child can use an iPad. A grandparent can FaceTime. That's the power of simplicity.

Speed reading works the same way. Simplicity is speed. When you try to remember every detail, you slow down. When you focus on the core message, you accelerate. Apple doesn't add features for the sake of adding them—they add only what's necessary. Speed readers do the same: they read only what's necessary. They skip the clutter and go straight to the meaning.

A technique from Apple's philosophy: "Less is more." Before reading, ask yourself: what is the one thing I need to understand from this text? Write it down. Then read with that one thing in mind. Ignore everything that doesn't serve that purpose. This laser focus will increase your speed dramatically, just as Apple's laser focus on user experience made their products legendary.

The next time you open a book, think like Apple. Keep it simple. Strip away the unnecessary. Focus on what matters. Your brain will thank you—and your reading speed will soar like the release of a new iPhone.
