Title: The Invisible Teacher

The best teacher you'll ever have is not a person — it's your own past self. Every book you've read, every conversation you've had, every mistake you've made is stored in your brain, waiting to be activated. Speed reading is simply the art of accessing that invisible teacher faster. When you read quickly, you're not learning from scratch; you're reminding yourself of what you already know.

Think of your brain as a vast library. You don't need to read every book on the shelf. You just need to know where to look. That's why previewing a text works so well. Spend one minute scanning the title, subtitles, first sentences, and any bold or italicized words. This tells your brain which "shelf" to go to. When you read the full text, your eyes move faster because your brain already has the map.

A simple practice: before reading, ask yourself, "What would I teach someone else about this topic right now?" This forces your brain to search its existing knowledge. Once it finds that knowledge, the new text becomes a conversation with your past self, not a lesson from a stranger. Familiarity breeds speed.

The next time you pick up something to read, remember: you're not a beginner. You've lived a lifetime of experiences. That is your invisible teacher. The faster you can call on that teacher, the faster you'll read. And the best part? This teacher gets better with every page you turn.
