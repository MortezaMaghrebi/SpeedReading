Title: The Patience of Prophet Ayyub (Job)

Prophet Ayyub (Job) is known for his extraordinary patience. He was a wealthy and healthy man, blessed with family and prosperity. But then he lost everything—his wealth, his children, and his health. He was left alone, afflicted with severe illness, and abandoned by most people. Yet, he never lost faith in God. He endured his trials for years, and in the end, God restored his health, wealth, and family.

What does the story of Prophet Ayyub teach us about speed reading? Patience. Speed reading is not about rushing—it's about trusting the process. When you first start, you may feel slow and frustrated. You may struggle to understand complex texts. That's your trial. But if you persist, if you keep practicing, your speed will improve. Just as Ayyub's patience was rewarded, your persistence in reading will be rewarded with fluency and speed.

A technique from Ayyub's story: don't give up when you face difficult passages. Instead, read them slowly, carefully, and with patience. Then move on. Trust that understanding will come with time. Ayyub didn't heal overnight—he healed gradually. Your reading speed will also improve gradually. Every practice session is a step toward recovery—not from illness, but from the habit of slow reading.

The next time you feel frustrated with a difficult text, remember Prophet Ayyub. He endured years of suffering with patience and faith. You can endure a few difficult paragraphs with the same patience. Trust the process. Keep reading. And in time, your speed and understanding will be restored—just as Ayyub's life was restored.
